Estimating climate impacts

An excellent review is Blanc and Reilly (2017). There are three broad approaches:

  • Cross-sectional estimation of production effect of temperature (Mendelsohn, Nordhaus, and Shaw 1994).
  • Estimate production using panel data with temperature as exogenous variable (Schlenker and Roberts 2009).
  • Crop simulation models of how crops respond to temperature changes (Antle and Stckle 2017).

Data

  • 4km grid of t-max, t-min, and precip. Historical: 1979-2015, Projected: 2006-2099 (Jenny Adam's Lab).
  • County level: ag land rent, crop and pasture acres, irrigated acres, income, population (NASS, census)

Method

Treatment effect: \[\tau = Y_{i1} - Y_{i0} \]

But \(Y_{i1}\) is unobserved. Instead we can estimate \(Y_{i1}\) by matching the characteristics for \(i\) based on a set of characteristics \(X_i\) (e.g. temperature, precipitation).

In essence: set \(Y_{i1} = Y_{j0}\), where \(j\) is the location most similar to \(i\) according to some distance metric.

Distance Metrics

  • Propensity score matching: good at minimizing discrepancy along the propensity score.

  • Mahalanobis distance matching: good at minimizing distance between individual coordinates of \(X\).

  • Genetic matching: search space over a weighted combination of the two. Enter R package Matching (Sekhon 2011).
